A regenerative braking system is an innovative technology used in electric and hybrid vehicles to improve energy efficiency. This system captures and converts the kinetic energy that is usually lost as heat during braking into electrical energy. This recovered energy is then stored in the vehicle's battery for later use, thereby increasing overall energy efficiency and reducing the need for frequent recharging or fuel consumption.

An ocean waste collecting device is an innovative tool designed to remove plastic and other debris from ocean waters. These devices often feature floating barriers and nets that capture waste without harming marine life. By collecting and removing pollutants, they help to protect aquatic ecosystems, reduce plastic pollution, and promote cleaner, healthier oceans. This technology plays a crucial role in addressing the growing environmental challenge of ocean waste, contributing to the preservation of marine biodiversity and the overall health of our planet.
